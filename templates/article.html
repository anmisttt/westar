What is Organic Farming?
Organic farming aims to provide the means of sustainable agriculture while applying environmentally friendly methods. The ultimate goal of organic farmers is to achieve maximum productivity while focusing on sustainable agriculture, re-establishing ecological balance, conserving flora and fauna, and contributing to the preservation of the soil by reducing the usage of chemicals and toxic substances in the farming process.
Another primary aim of organic farming is to grow healthier and more nutritious crops for consumers. It is essential to note that organic farming avoids the use of pesticides, synthetic fertilizers, antibiotics, growth hormones, and genetically modified seeds.

Organic farming practices

Organic farmers put into practice several methods to achieve environmental and agricultural sustainability. Here are some of the most common practices among them:

Soil maintenance Farmers preserve the health of the soil by using compost or manure instead of synthetic fertilizers. The usage of organic fertilizers improves soil quality as it enables it to retain better air, nutrients, and moisture which results in healthier, thriving plants and crops.

Crop rotation Growing the same plants in the same soil year after year decreases the quality of the soil. By growing different plants every year, organic farmers ensure that the soil is more abundant in nutrients acquired by the various crops that are produced in it.

Cover crops Cover crops are non-income generating crops planted in between growth cycles or in some cases together with the main crops. Cover crops enrich the soil because they improve the soil’s chemical and biological properties as well as reduce erosion.

Buffer zones Buffer zones are designated areas of crops organic farmers plant between organically certified fields and non-organic land. In this way, they ensure that there is no contamination between organic and non-organic soil.
